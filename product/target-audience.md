# Target Audience

## Primary Demographics

- **Age:** 18-35 (core: 22-30)
- **Gender:** Primarily women (75%+), but inclusive of all
- **Location:** US, UK, Australia, Canada, Germany
- **Tech Savvy:** Moderate — comfortable with apps, active on social media
- **Platform:** iOS primary, Android secondary

## User Personas

### 1. "The 75 Hard Survivor" (Primary)
**Who:** Tried 75 Hard 1-3 times and quit or burned out
**Pain:** Feels like a failure for not completing it. Frustrated by restart rule.
**Desire:** Structure and accountability without the extreme demands
**Message:** "You didn't fail. The challenge failed you. Try 75 Soft."

### 2. "The Wellness Curious" (Secondary)
**Who:** New to wellness challenges, intimidated by 75 Hard
**Pain:** Wants to build habits but doesn't know where to start
**Desire:** A gentle, guided entry into wellness
**Message:** "Your first step doesn't have to be extreme. Start soft."

### 3. "The Journaling Enthusiast"
**Who:** Loves reflection, mood tracking, and personal growth
**Pain:** Existing habit trackers are cold and functional
**Desire:** A beautiful, emotional experience that feeds the soul
**Message:** "More than a tracker. It's a journal for your wellness journey."

### 4. "The Visual Motivator"
**Who:** Motivated by seeing visual progress and rewards
**Pain:** Checkboxes don't excite them
**Desire:** Beautiful, tangible proof of their consistency
**Message:** "Watch your garden grow, flower by flower, day by day."

### 5. "The Self-Care Advocate"
**Who:** Practices self-care, values mental health and rest
**Pain:** Fitness culture is too aggressive and guilt-driven
**Desire:** An app that aligns with their values of compassion
**Message:** "Finally, a wellness app that believes rest is sacred."

### 6. "The Fresh Starter" (Seasonal)
**Who:** New Year's resolution makers, "Monday starters"
**Pain:** Past attempts at habit building failed
**Desire:** A fresh, doable plan that actually sticks
**Message:** "75 days. 4 habits. No perfection needed."

## Psychographic Profile

### Values
- Self-improvement but not at the expense of mental health
- Balance over extremes
- Community and support
- Aesthetics and beauty in daily life
- Authenticity and vulnerability

### Content Consumption
- **TikTok** (primary) — wellness, self-care, productivity, "day in my life"
- **Instagram** — aesthetic feeds, stories, Reels
- **YouTube** — wellness vlogs, productivity, self-improvement
- **Podcasts** — wellness, personal growth, mental health

### Buying Behavior
- Willing to pay for quality wellness apps ($4.99-9.99/mo)
- Values free trials before committing
- Influenced by TikTok recommendations and reviews
- Responds to aesthetic marketing and emotional messaging
- Prefers apps that feel personal, not corporate

## Pain Points to Address in Marketing

1. **"I keep failing at habit challenges"** → No restart rule eliminates failure
2. **"Fitness apps make me feel guilty"** → Bambloom celebrates, never shames
3. **"75 Hard is unrealistic"** → 75 Soft is designed for real life
4. **"Habit trackers are boring"** → Flower garden makes it beautiful
5. **"I need accountability but hate pressure"** → Panda is supportive, not pushy
6. **"I don't see my progress"** → Journey path and garden show every day

## Emotional Triggers for Video Content

- **Relief:** "Finally, something that works WITH me"
- **Hope:** "I can actually do this"
- **Belonging:** "This was made for people like me"
- **Excitement:** "This app is SO cute"
- **Pride:** "Look at my garden after 30 days"
- **Validation:** "It's okay to rest. It's okay to not be perfect."
